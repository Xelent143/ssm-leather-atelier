# MOTOGRIP GEAR Brand Identity

## Brand Platform

Name: MOTOGRIP GEAR

Tagline: Road armor, cut to grip.

Positioning: Premium motorcycle leather gear for riders who want protection, fit, and restraint in the same piece. MOTOGRIP GEAR sits between technical road equipment and luxury leather apparel: purpose-built enough for the ride, refined enough off the bike.

Brand promise: Leather gear that moves with the rider, holds its shape, and can be tuned to the body.

Core audience: Riders, collectors, and style-led customers buying jackets, vests, and leather trousers who care about fit, hardware, construction, and visual consistency.

## Pillars

Grip: Every product should feel anchored, tactile, and controlled.

Guard: Protective language without panic. Reinforced, repairable, road-ready.

Motion: Copy and imagery should suggest lean, reach, speed, wind, and weather.

Precision: Measurements, hardware, panels, seams, and fit notes are part of the luxury.

## Brand Voice

Voice traits:
- Direct, controlled, and confident.
- Technical without becoming cold.
- Premium without sounding delicate.
- Rider-aware, never costume-like.

Use words like:
- road-cut
- reinforced
- measured
- grip
- reach
- patina
- hardware
- fit room
- road armor
- made to measure

Avoid words like:
- boutique
- cute
- edgy
- rebel lifestyle
- biker cosplay
- handcrafted poetry
- delicate

Sample lines:
- Built for the lean. Cut for the grip.
- Road armor with a cleaner line.
- Stock sizing when it works. Made to measure when it matters.
- Shoulder room for the reach. Structure for everything after.
- Leather that earns its patina on the road.

## Logo System

Primary logo: MOTOGRIP wordmark with shield-road monogram.

Icon mark: Angular shield with diagonal road lines and an abstract MG. Use when space is tight: favicon, mobile header, product badge, care card.

Clearspace: Keep at least the height of the shield mark around the full logo.

Minimum sizes:
- Full logo: 150 px wide digital.
- Icon mark: 24 px digital.

Do:
- Use on bone, warm ivory, pale concrete, graphite, or transparent backgrounds.
- Pair the logo with uppercase technical captions.
- Keep the mark sharp and angular.

Do not:
- Stretch the logo.
- Add glow, bevel, flames, wings, skulls, or extra motorcycle cliches.
- Place the logo over busy product details unless there is a clean light field or controlled scrim.

## Color Palette

Warm Ivory: `#F5F1E8`

Road Bone: `#ECE5D3`

Pale Concrete: `#E0D6BF`

Ink Black: `#161A1C`

Graphite: `#252A2D`

Worn Steel: `#AAB1B4`

Signal Orange: `#FF5A1F`

Brake Red: `#A9271D`

Use Signal Orange as the primary action and highlight color against the light base. Use Brake Red sparingly for urgency, low stock, or editorial accents.

## Typography

Display direction: condensed, engineered, uppercase-friendly sans. The current site can use Archivo as the MOTOGRIP display mode.

Body direction: quiet, readable sans with compact line heights for product and fit information.

Caption direction: mono technical labels for specs, sizes, measurements, order states, and material notes.

## Consistency Rules

- Keep the background light and neutral in 80 percent of imagery.
- Keep leather texture visible; avoid overly smooth surfaces.
- Product images should show the garment first, atmosphere second.
- Use signal orange as an accent, not as a dominant wash.
- Do not generate visible brand text on products unless using the supplied logo asset.
- For product grids, lock angle, crop, and light direction across all items.
- For PDP galleries, use this order: hero, back/side angle, detail macro, fit/measurement detail, lifestyle context.
